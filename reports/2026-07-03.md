## Match cards

### Australia vs Egypt — Away (Egypt), 1-2, conf 2
Market shades Egypt in a near-even three-way (27/33/40), but the swing factor is
murkier than a day ago: coach Hossam Hassan confirmed Mohamed Salah is fit to
feature yet would **not** commit to starting him after his hamstring scare, so a
managed/bench role is live. With Fatouh out and Salah's minutes uncertain,
Egypt's quality edge over an organised, set-piece-reliant Australia is thinner
than the line. Close to a coin-flip; if Salah starts on the bench the draw
(~33%) is very live. High penalty risk.

- [Spokesman — Salah fit but maybe not from the start](https://www.spokesman.com/stories/2026/jul/02/egypt-star-mohamed-salah-fit-to-face-australia-but/)

### Argentina vs Cape Verde — Home, 3-0, conf 5
Overwhelming favourites (~84%). Messi and Mac Allister return to the XI, De Paul
is back and Romero should start despite a minor knee knock. Cape Verde are
defensively resilient but lack the firepower to trouble the holders; Messi has
scored in seven straight WC matches. Controlled Argentina win.
- [Sports Mole — Messi returns, Romero boost, predicted XI](https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/argentina/world-cup-2026/predicted-lineups/messi-returns-romero-boost-predicted-argentina-xi-vs-cape-verde_600514.html)

### Colombia vs Ghana — Home, 2-0, conf 4
Colombia firmed over the last day (63%→67%), won Group K ahead of Portugal and
reinstate Muñoz, Mojica and Luis Suárez with no fresh injury concerns. Ghana
scraped through as a best third-placed side and carry fitness doubts (Semenyo's
ankle, a likely keeper change with Ati-Zigi's groin). Colombia's quality and
health point to a comfortable win, with Ghana's pace on the break the main
threat.
- [Sports Mole — preview, team news, lineups](https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/colombia/world-cup-2026/preview/colombia-vs-ghana-prediction-team-news-lineups_600509.html)

### Canada vs Morocco — Away (Morocco), 1-2, conf 3
World-class Morocco are clear favourites (~54%) with a clean injury sheet —
Bounou in goal, Hakimi at right-back. Canada's unpriced boost is Alphonso
Davies, tipped for his first tournament start behind top scorer Jonathan David
after returning off the bench vs South Africa, which keeps the hosts live at
home. Hakimi vs a starting Davies is the pivotal flank battle. Penalty risk.
- [Sports Mole — 'Davies or no Davies' predicted Canada XI](https://www.sportsmole.co.uk/football/canada/world-cup-2026/predicted-lineups/davies-or-no-davies-predicted-canada-xi-vs-morocco_600550.html)

### Paraguay vs France — Away (France), 0-2, conf 5
France overwhelming favourites (~81%) and Deschamps is unlikely to change the XI
that won 3-0 over Sweden — Mbappé (chasing Messi's all-time WC scoring record)
leads a Dembélé-Olise-Barcola front line. Thuram is doubtful (calf) and Kanté a
bench option, but that barely dents the tournament's most dangerous attack.
Paraguay stunned Germany on penalties and get Diego Gómez back from suspension,
but ranked 41st they lack the firepower; a France win to nil is likeliest.
Their only route is the low block and penalties.
- [ESPN — Paraguay vs France how to watch & predicted line-ups](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/49233642/paraguay-vs-france-fifa-world-cup-2026-tv-channel-how-watch-kick-live-stream-injury-predicted-line-ups)

### Brazil vs Norway — Home, 2-1, conf 3
Market makes Brazil favourites only modestly (51/26/23). Estêvão is out of the
tournament and Neymar is a bench-only fitness case, but crucially Vinícius Jr
and Rodrygo are both fit and expected to start — a healthier attack than a few
days ago, with Bruno Guimarães leading the tournament in assists. Norway are the
round's most live underdog: Haaland has five goals in three WC games and struck
late to beat Ivory Coast. Brazil edge a tight one; the extra-time/Haaland angle
is the clearest against-the-favourite lean of the round.
- [Squawka — Brazil vs Norway R16 preview](https://www.squawka.com/us/news/world-cup/match-preview-brazil-vs-norway-07-05-26-world-cup-2026-round-of-16/)

### Mexico vs England — Away (England), 1-2, conf 2
Market shades England as narrow favourites in a tight three-way (31/30/39) on
Tuchel's organisation and Kane's finishing (13 WC goals). But this is a genuine
trap game: it is played at the Estadio Azteca (~2,200m), where Mexico are
unbeaten at World Cups, and El Tri arrive on four straight clean sheets and
their first knockout win in 40 years. Slim lean to England's quality, tempered
by altitude and a hostile crowd; the draw is very live, and Bellingham is one
booking from a quarter-final suspension.

### Portugal vs Spain — Away (Spain), 1-2, conf 4
Market makes Spain clear favourites (~50%) in this Iberian derby and the
underlying numbers agree: Portugal have badly underperformed their xG (~0.4
npxG vs Croatia, ~0.47 vs Colombia) and needed a Ronaldo penalty to survive
Croatia, while Spain's Rodri/Pedri midfield and elite defence (under 0.2
post-shot xG a game) are the tournament's best. Yamal is fit and in form; Nico
Williams is managed but available. Spain's control edges it. Note: Ronaldo (41)
has never scored in a WC knockout in eight attempts.
- [BetMGM — Spain vs Portugal prediction, odds & preview](https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/world-cup/spain-vs-portugal-prediction-odds-preview-world-cup-july-6-bm16/)

### USA vs Belgium — Away (Belgium), 1-2, conf 2
The market has the co-hosts as the thinnest of favourites (37/28/35), largely on
the Seattle crowd. I lean the other way: leading scorer Folarin Balogun is
**suspended** (straight red vs Bosnia, no appeal), removing the USA's main
finisher, and likely replacement Ricardo Pepi has struggled under Pochettino.
Belgium beat the USA 5-2 three months ago, the USMNT haven't beaten them since
1930, and Courtois-De Bruyne-Doku-Trossard is the superior spine. Pulisic's
return helps, but Belgium's quality edges a genuine 50/50. **Deliberate
deviation from the razor-thin market favourite.** High penalty risk.
