## Match cards

### France vs Morocco — Pick: France (2-0), confidence 4
France are firm regulation favourites at 61% and have been the tournament's most
complete side — five wins from five, 14 scored and only two conceded, with Mbappe
(7 goals) fit behind a settled Kante–Tchouameni–Rabiot midfield. Morocco are
unbeaten and superbly organised, genuinely dangerous on the counter, so the goals
should stay low — but the class gap and France's control point to Les Bleus.

### Spain vs Belgium — Pick: Spain (2-0), confidence 4
The market makes Spain a clear 58% and the profile backs it: four straight clean
sheets since the opening 0-0 with Cape Verde (Uruguay, Saudi Arabia, Austria,
Portugal all beaten), Oyarzabal leading the scoring charts and a Rodri–Pedri–Fabian
midfield controlling games. Belgium arrive off a 4-1 rout of the USA with real menace
in De Bruyne, Lukaku and Doku, but they drew three group games; Spain's defensive
record and possession should carry a tight tie.

### Norway vs England — Pick: England (1-2), confidence 3
England are the market call at 51% and the line firmed slightly this week, backed by
a deeper squad — though nothing has been comfortable. A concrete edge may be unpriced:
a flu bug has spread through Norway's camp (manager Solbakken confirmed it), even if
Haaland — who scored twice to sink Brazil — is expected fit. Haaland can win it
single-handedly so this stays live, but England's depth plus Norway's illness
disruption edge it, with real extra-time/penalty risk.
- **Disagreement:** Haaland's form makes this far more live than a 51% away line implies; a shootout is on the table.

### Argentina vs Switzerland — Pick: Argentina (2-0), confidence 4
The defending champions are a solid 56% favourite and the line firmed toward them
across the week, with Messi in extraordinary form — eight goals and leading the Golden
Boot — behind a squad with quality in every department. Switzerland are organised and
ground out a 0-0 (penalties) over Colombia to reach their deepest run since 1954, and
will try to congest midfield and force Argentina wide; but the gulf in match-winners
points to Argentina, likely by a low-scoring margin.
- **Disagreement:** Switzerland's discipline (clean sheet over Colombia) makes a cagey, tight game into the closing stages plausible.
