## Norway vs England — QF (kick-off 21:00 UTC)

**Pick: Away (England) · Score 1-2 · Confidence 3/5**

England are the market call at 50% for a place in the semis, and the Opta
supercomputer agrees almost exactly (50.4% England in regulation, 25.1% Norway,
24.6% extra time). Kane leads the tournament conversation with six goals, backed by
Bellingham (four) and England's superior squad depth. But Norway are in their first-ever
quarter-final riding an Erling Haaland who has scored in every one of his four World Cup
games — seven goals already, including the double that knocked out Brazil — and a striker
in that form keeps a supposedly one-sided tie firmly alive. England's depth should edge it,
but with genuine extra-time/penalty risk against a man who can win it alone.

- **Disagreement flag:** Bettors keep backing Norway as the round's upset pick; a Haaland-led shootout is very much on the table.
- **Line movement:** From yesterday the line drifted a fraction toward Norway (England away 51.0% → 49.6%), but the picture is essentially unchanged.

## Argentina vs Switzerland — QF (kick-off 01:00 UTC, 12 Jul)

**Pick: Home (Argentina) · Score 2-0 · Confidence 4/5**

The defending champions are a solid 57% market favourite with Messi in extraordinary
form — eight goals and leading the Golden Boot race despite missing two penalties — behind
a settled, deep XI. Switzerland have been the tournament's stealth story, out of the group
unscathed and conceding zero in regulation across two knockout wins before edging Colombia
on penalties, but they are sweating on the fitness of Manzambi (knee), Jaquez and Aebischer.
Argentina's own path has been jittery (extra time vs Cape Verde, 3-2 escapes over Egypt),
so a cagey game is plausible — but the gulf in match-winners points to Argentina by a
low-scoring margin.

- **Disagreement flag:** Switzerland's Xhaka–Freuler screen makes a smothering low-block into the closing stages a real risk if they neutralise Messi and Mac Allister.
- **Line movement:** Flat versus yesterday (Argentina 56.4% → 56.8%).

## France vs Spain — SF (kick-off 19:00 UTC, 14 Jul)

**Pick: Home (France) · Score 2-1 · Confidence 3/5**

The market makes France a narrow favourite (41% vs Spain 30%, with 29% on the draw over
90 minutes), and the profile backs a slight lean: France are a perfect 6-0-0, dismantling
opponents with Mbappé, an in-form Dembélé (five goals) and Michael Olise's tournament-high
six assists. Spain have ground through — five of their wins by a single goal, two settled
after the 85th minute — and their record six-match clean-sheet streak was finally broken by
Belgium, while Yamal has gone quiet since the group stage. Rodri is back near his best to
anchor Spain's midfield, and they beat this same France side 2-1 in the Euro 2024 semi, so
the gap is small; France's form and cutting edge just tip a very tight tie, with real
penalty risk in a no-draw knockout.

- **Injury watch:** Tchouaméni (groin) is questionable for France after sitting out the Morocco QF; Mbappé took a late knock vs Morocco but it looked precautionary and he is expected to feature. Spain's Yéremy Pino is likely out (collar bone).
- **Disagreement flag:** Spain's knockout resilience and the Euro 2024 head-to-head are the case against the market — this is close to a coin-flip dressed as a 41/30.
